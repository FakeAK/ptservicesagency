## Understanding the Schengen VISA

The Schengen visa allows travelers to visit most of the European countries with a single visa. This visa allows a temporary visit of up to 90 days in any 180-day period to any country in the Schengen area. With the recent arrival of Bulgaria and Romania in the Schengen area, the Schengen visa now covers 27 countries, including popular destinations like France, Germany, Italy, and Spain. This unified visa system simplifies travel within Europe, making it an attractive option for tourists.

The Schengen visa comes in 3 forms:

- **Single entry visa** - This visa allows you to enter the Schengen area once.
- **Multiple-entry visa** - This visa allows your to go several times to the Schengen area for as long as the visa is valid.
- **Airport transit visa** - Allows you to connect through the international transit area of an airport in the Schengen area during a stopover or a change of flights. It does not allow you to leave the international transit area.

## Which Schengen VISA Do I need?

Depending on the reason you come to Europe, you'll want a different type of VISA. The are many type of Schengen visa but the most common are:

- **Tourist visa** - This visa is solely for the purpose of leisure travel including vacationing, cruising, sightseeing and other recreational activities.
- **Business visa** - A business visa is required if during any portion of your visit you intend to conduct business or commercial activities, e.g. meetings, conferences, training, trade fairs.
- **Airport transit** - Depending on your passport issuing country, you might need a transit visa to change aircraft at an airport in a Schengen country.
- **Visiting family / friends** - If you are traveling to Europe to visit family / friends (including girlfriend/wife and boyfriend/husband), you may apply for this visa.

## Which Documents Are Necessary to Obtain a Schengen Visa?

Several documents are needed to obtain the Schengen visa.

- A valid passport. The passport expiry date should be at least 3 months after the date of your departure from the Schengen area. For multiple-entry visas, the expiry date should be at least 3 months after your departure from the last country visited
- A visa application form.
- A photo in compliance with ICAO standards.
- A medical insurance covering emergency medical care, hospitalisation and repatriation (including in the event of death).
- Supporting documents relating to the purpose of your stay, evidence of financial means and accommodation during your stay, and evidence of your intention to return to your home country after your stay.
- Your fingerprints - They will be collected when you submit your application (exemptions exist for specific categories of applicants).

## How to Obtain a Schengen VISA in Thailand?

You will need to gather all your necessary documents before starting your Schengen VISA application. Depending on your destination country, you will book an appointment either at VFS Global or TLSContact in Bangkok. Keep in mind that both VFS Global and TLSContact charge fees for processing your documents.

During your appointment, you will submit your documents along with your passport and provide your fingerprints. Depending on the country you are applying to, you may be required to attend an interview where you will answer questions about your trip. If you have enlisted the services of a VISA agency, they will help you prepare for this interview.

After the interview, you will need to pay the VISA fees and indicate whether you would like to collect your passport with the VISA affixed or have it sent to you. Using a VISA agency can streamline this process, ensuring that all steps are completed accurately and efficiently.

## How Long Does The Process Take to be Processed?

You'll typically receive a decision on your application within 15 calendar days. However, in some cases, it might take longer, extending up to 45 calendar days.

## How Much Does It Cost to Obtain a Schengen VISA

The cost of the visa has recently increased (since June 11). The prices are now 90 EUR for adults and 45 EUR for children aged 6-12. An additional fee may apply if you go through visa service centres, which collect applications on behalf of consulates in some cases. The visa fee can be waived for specific categories of applicants.
